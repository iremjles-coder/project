Respiratory System

Nose: The nose filters, warms, and humidifies air, but can be affected by rhinitis and nasal polyps. The nose also contains tiny hairs called cilia that help trap dust and particles. Allergies and sinus infections can also impact nasal function.

Trachea (Windpipe): The trachea carries air to the lungs and can experience tracheitis and tracheal stenosis. It's lined with mucous membranes that help trap particles. The trachea is also supported by cartilage rings to keep it open.

Bronchi: The bronchi carry air to the lungs, but are prone to bronchitis and bronchospasm. Bronchi also have smooth muscle that can tighten in conditions like asthma. They branch into smaller airways called bronchioles.

Lungs: The lungs exchange oxygen and carbon dioxide, but are susceptible to pneumonia infection, COPD (chronic obstruction), and lung cancer. Lungs have tiny air sacs called alveoli where gas exchange happens. They're also covered by a protective layer called pleura.

Diaphragm: The diaphragm helps breathing, but can be affected by diaphragmatic hernia and diaphragm paralysis. It's a dome-shaped muscle that contracts to expand the chest cavity. The diaphragm is crucial for efficient breathing and separates the chest from the abdome
